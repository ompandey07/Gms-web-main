Brief Introduction of BSW course in Nepal ( Introduction to BSW, Bsw colleges in Nepal, Scope of BSW in Nepal, Cost to complete the degree, facts and detailed insight of the BSW course)

Bachelor of social work (BSW) is one of the subject for study, on demand, in the recent days in Nepal as well as all around the globe. With the growing involvement of International nongovernmental organization (INGO) and nongovernmental organization (NGO) for the upbringing of people’s lives and the country, there has been a demand in aspirants from the field of social work.BSW course is designed to fulfill the demand of social workers and activists for those noble purposes. Better reward, international exposure and stability are some aspects of NGO and INGO which is driving young Nepal for social work as a profession which has been a favorite after career involvement. BSW prepares the student for entry-level practices in the field of social work and helping progress people’s lives.

BSW course in Nepali Colleges/ Universities

BA in Social studies (BSW ) is a 36 months fully fledged degree programme offered by Tribhuvan University, Kathmandu University, Pokhara University and Purbanchal University. The programme helps student inculcate day-to-day skills in themselves to help people in building a better relationship, in overcoming family and societal problems, in uplifting livelihood and their environment and many others. When it comes to BSW in Nepal the colleges educate students to focus on individual and social problems that arise in the civilization which includes violence of a different kind, abuses, job difficulties, displacement issues, political issues, migration issues, medical attention and many others.

BSW in Kathmandu University and BSW in Tribhuwan University ( KU BSW , TU BSW)

  1. Duration: 36 months
  2. Evaluation method: GPA

Eligibility to study BSW / Requirements to Join

To be eligible to appear for the University examinations at the end of each semester, a minimum of 80% attendance in class as well as in the fieldwork is a must. Other academic requirements include:

  • assignment
  • Research Projects and presentation
  • Seminar papers and presentation
  • Fieldwork is taken as essential element for professional training in Social Work.
  • Students are required to contribute minimum fifteen hours of field work every week

Career Opportunity/Job Prospects:

Social Work graduates have scope in various fields from government, private organizations, NGOs and INGOs. Following are the list with career opportunity they might opt for:

  • Degree under Social Sciences
  • Career as Social Worker
  • Entrepreneurship
  • Social Work Institutions
  • Social Work Lecturers
  • Government Offices, NGOs and INGOs

  • Introduction to Psychology

Second year

  • PSY 423-ADOLESCENCE AND JUVENILE DELINQUENCY

  • SW 424A-SOCIAL CASE WORK PRACTICE
  • SW 424B-SOCIAL WORK PRACTICE WITH GROUPS
  • अनिवार्य नेपाली ( कोड नं. ४०२)

Third Year

  • SW 425A-Community Organization
  • PSY 425-Psychosocial Counselling
  • RDS 410-Development Practices(Elective)
  • JMC 410-Introduction to Mass Communication(Elective)
  • COMP.ENG 403-Compulsary English
